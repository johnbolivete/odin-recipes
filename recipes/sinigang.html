Description

Sinigang, it is popular because of a combination of sour and saltyness. it is relaxing when you sipped the soup especially when hot.

locals have different recipes for this but sour taste is not excluded

Ingredients

  • 1 kg pork belly or pork ribs, cut into chunks
  • 8 cups water
  • 1 medium onion, quartered
  • 2 medium tomatoes, quartered
  • 1 radish (labanos), sliced into rounds
  • 1 bundle string beans (sitaw), cut into 2-inch pieces
  • 1 medium eggplant, sliced
  • 1 cup kangkong (water spinach) or spinach
  • 1 packet tamarind soup mix (or 1/2 cup fresh tamarind juice)
  • 2 green chilies (siling haba) (optional)
  • 2 tablespoons fish sauce (patis)

Step by step

  1. Boil the pork:

    2. In a large pot, bring the water to a boil. Add the pork, onion, and tomatoes.

    Simmer for 45 minutes to 1 hour, or until the pork becomes tender. Skim off any foam or impurities from the surface.

  2. Add the vegetables:

    3. Add the radish and simmer for 5 minutes.

    Add the eggplant and string beans, cooking for another 5 minutes.

  3. Season the soup:

    4. Add the tamarind soup mix or fresh tamarind juice, and stir well. Adjust the sourness to your taste.

  4. Enhance the flavor

    5. Add fish sauce, green chilies (if using), and salt to taste. Simmer for 2-3 minutes.

  5. 5. Add the leafy greens:

    6. Turn off the heat and add the kangkong or spinach. Let it wilt in the residual heat.

This comforting, sour soup is a Filipino favorite, perfect for rainy days or family gatherings!
